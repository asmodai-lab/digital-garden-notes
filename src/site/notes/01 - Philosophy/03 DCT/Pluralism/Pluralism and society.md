## Multi-faith societies
- in the 2011 census, 59% were Christian, 25% atheist, and 5% Muslim; whilst Christianity is still the dominant religious group in the UK, other religious groups are growing. In some local areas, Christians and atheists may find themselves in the minority.
- with globalisation, spreading in the second half of the 20th century faster than ever before, comes the ability to encounter people of other faiths
- Globalisation has generally led to tolerance in society of other faiths; the trend of this tolerance arguably started with the Enlightenment, but has been facilitated by modern lifestyle

### Inter-faith dialogue
1. Migration has led the normalisation of communities of those from other cultures in the UK, thus contributing to our multi-faith society
2. **David Ford, *'The future of Christian theology***, speaks about the Holocaust and how it has forced Christians to think about its relationship with Judaism, as a result of institutionalised antisemitism:
	- ***In 2014, Pope Francis visited the National Holocaust Museum*** in Jerusalem, as part of an on-going attempt to build bridges between the Catholic Church and Judaism
	- [[01 - Philosophy/03 DCT/Pluralism/Inter-faith dialogue#Dabru Emet\|Dabru Emet]] — this came about when over 200 Rabbis issued a statement about the shared ground between Jews and Christians, "*We believe it is time for Jews to learn about the efforts of Christians to honour Judaism*"
	- The statement came up with claims such as Jews and Christians worship the same God, that Christians can respect the claim of the Jews on the land of Israel, and that Nazism was not a Christian phenomenon
3. Rising islamophobia, following 9/11 and 7/7
- In 2007, leading Muslims wrote a letter called [[01 - Philosophy/03 DCT/Pluralism/Inter-faith dialogue#A common word between us and you\|A common word between us and you]] — the aim of the book and related activities was to stress the **common ground between Muslims and Christians.** It pointed out that there was more uniting the two faiths than dividing them.
- **John Piper: *The common ground identified is too weak***— Love of God, prophet who says to love your enemies; misses the true point of the religion. Muslims to not agree that Jesus was God & died for our sins — "*If you reject me, you reject the one who sent me*" (Luke), "*At best, it's misleading*" — John Piper suggesting that it is deceitful to turn against the true meaning of Christianity

#### [[01 - Philosophy/03 DCT/Pluralism/Inter-faith dialogue#"Sharing the Gospel of salvation"\|Sharing the Gospel of Salvation]]
- the Church of England synod from 2010 recognises that modern Britain is a multi-faith society, yet also affirms that salvation has been achieved through the Christ-event and thus Christians must proclaim this
1. **Christ**: Uniqueness of Christ in a multi-faith society -> salvation is through Jesus alone
2. **Sharing Gospel**: Being a Christian disciple means sharing the gospel
3. **Sensitivity**: it also acknowledges that missionary work and imperialism can be confused together; one must be sensitive to those around them and engage positively with other communities
4. **Distinctivity and pride**: Anglicanism is facing a challenge, where it has become a minority faith; live good Christian lifestyles as distinct and authentic Christians, being proud of your tradition
	- "The stronger sense of difference and diversity — including religious diversity — in Britain today has challenged us to become a more generous culture. Sadly, it has also made many nervous of publicly espousing a vision of the common good for all people."

#### *Redemptoris Missio*
- Redemptoris Missio is a papal encyclical (authoritative letter from the Pope, settling a contentious issue within the Church) written in 1990 by Pope John Paul II
	- it focusses on the missionary work of the Church in the modern, multi-faith world
- Whilst it recognises the changed world, it re-affirms the need for the Christian Church to evangelise, and the importance of never shying away from the Gospel

1. **Christianity**: Christ is the only true saviour, inter-faith dialogue allows one to share the full account of Christian beliefs
	- "*Faith is strengthened when it is given to others!*"
2. **Missionary**: The Holy Spirit, sent at Pentecost, is there to encourage disciples to evangelise others, to win their souls for Christ — mission is for all members of the Church, not just priests: *"peoples everywhere, open the doors to Christ!"*
3. **Respectful**: But it also recognises that, in a modern world, missionary work can be seen as imperialism — Christians have to be respectful and mindful of other faiths and cultures
4. **Love**: love and hope should be the basis for inter-faith dialogue; key aim is to build a happy society
5. **Truths**: all religions hold aspects of the truth so dialogue needs to be respectful (i.e. no ulterior motive of conversion)
6. **Learning**: dialogue, as is suggested, is a two-way street; just as other faiths can learn from Christianity, Christianity can learn from other faiths

## Scriptural reasoning movement
- began in the USA and has been adapted by the Cambridge inter-faith programme
- it's purpose is NOT to convert anyone, but to help those from different faith backgrounds to understand where and why they disagree about different truth claims
- the movement started with religions based on books —  i.e. the abrahamic religions, because of their shared history: but it has also successfully worked with people from other faith backgrounds

### Methods
- sessions will focus on one text from each tradition, with the facilitator leading a discussion of the messages
- understand in two ways: as texts in themselves (historic context, language), as being read (context of modern life)
- participants encouraged not to speak "for" their faith but to say "as a Christian, this text says X"

### Aims
- maintains the spirit of dialogue, no attempt to convert people
	1. **Wisdom**: united by their desire to gain wisdom out of a discussion
	2. **Collegiality**: all participants and contributions are equal
	3. **Hospitality**: non-judgemental

### Evaluation
- <span style="color:rgb(146, 208, 80)">'post-liberal' theological movement, where truth is less of a concern than mutual understanding — helps build respect and understanding between different religions</span>
- <span style="color:rgb(255, 77, 77)">neutral approach towards scripture has been argued to be misleading within Christianity — revelation through Christ should mean a Christocentric position towards the Bible</span>
- <span style="color:rgb(255, 77, 77)">Relativises or 'flattens' Christianity in the process of engaging with other religions — accepts multiple truths and disregards the uniqueness of the Father-Son relationship and the Christ-event</span>

## What is the value of inter-faith dialogue?
- vital part of Jesus' call to reconciliation and 'love thy neighbour'
- in a multi-cultural country, many see the dialogue as helping social cohesion and preventing conflict
- many believers in inter-faith dialogue argue religions often have similar core principles
- many see it as an important part of evangelism — for many Christians, missionary work is a process of holistic engagement through involvement in community and charity, as opposed to encroachment upon other religions

### Different approaches
1. **Exclusivist** -> use it as an opportunity to convert others, as they believe only their religion has real insight into salvation
2. **Inclusivist** -> may engage in order to address criticisms of exclusivist Christianity being too domineering; may look also for critical insight from other religions, knowing they also hold truths
3. **Pluralists** -> freely call for critical judgement of their own religion, developing a global faith
