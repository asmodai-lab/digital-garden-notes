## Rejection of the state
- the state is immoral as it rules by coercion and deceit
- it is unjust in it's defence of economic inequality.

## Liberty (rejection of authority)
- liberty means being free from the compulsory state to pursue and explore one's choices
- individuals only achieve liberty when they can pursue their individuality or where power is shared equally in society.
- Liberty is incompatible with any form of political authority

## Anarchy is order
- contrary to the widely held view that anarchy means chaos, anarchists argue that anarchy represents order
- the state encourages humans to be aggressive and greedy
- only without a state can humans create a society based on natural order

## Economic freedom
- the economy should be a space where free individuals can manage their own affairs without state ownership or regulation
	- collectivist anarchism criticize the state for upholding an exploitative system
	- anarcho-capitalists criticise the state for perverting the true nature of the market

## Utopian
- to consider the benefits of an idealized society in order to develop a critique of existing society
- a belief in the unlimited potential and natural goodness of humankind
- to consider the criticisms levelled against anarchist as unachievable and unrealistic
