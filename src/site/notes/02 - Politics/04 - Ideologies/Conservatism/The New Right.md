## Neo-conservatism


### Principles
#### Personal responsibility
- neo-cons place a lot of emphasis on the idea of personal responsibility
- they reject the socialist idea that human nature is determined by economic and social conditions. Instead, they argue that through enough hard work, in a Western society, people can be successful.
- therefore, poverty and unemployment are understood to be in some part an individual choice, and potentially the result of weak morals.
- At the 1981 Conservative Party Conference, Norman Tebbit was the Employment secretary. In the wake of riots in the poorer parts of Birmingham and Brixton, be have a speech about his unemployed father in the 1930s. He said:
	- *"He didn't riot. he got on his bike and looked for work, and he kept looking till he found it!"*
- Margaret Thatcher also believed that people were blaming 'society' for their problems. In 1987, she said:

#### Duty to be loyal
The notion of duty to *your* group permeates neo-conservative thinking.
- firstly, partners should be romantically loyal to one another. This maintains nuclear families. Neo-conservatives suggest that broken homes harm children and that single parent families are linked to poorer educational outcomes and greater risks of criminality. The state should discourage divorce and encourage marriage
- Secondly, children should be loyal to authority figures. Part of the justification is Biblical — *'honour thy father and thy mother'* — but also because rebellious behaviour often leads to damaging vices: drug taking, petty criminality, anti-social behaviour, teenage pregnancies, etc.
- Thirdly, people should be loyal to their nation. people should celebrate the historical successes of their country. People emigrating to a country should adopt its culture, and reject any aspects of their previous culture which clash with that of the new nation. The nation — not class, religion, gender, sexuality, etc — should be the primary political allegiance. This is because it fosters a sense of 'togetherness' with neighbours, which limits immoral actions that harm other people. Other allegiances may lead you to harm your neighbour.
- Finally, leaders should be loyal to their groups. Strong leadership, 'doing the right thing', and refusing to compromise if it will harm your group's members is to be encouraged, as it fosters the common identity. A lack of common identity leads to disorder.


## Comparison

| Atomistic                                                                | Patriotic.                                                            |
| Libertarian                                                              | Authoritarian                                                         |
