## One nation conservatism questions

### History 

1. Where does the name One Nation Conservatism derive from? Who coined the term, and how did he use it? 

2. What did he consider the two separate nations to be? What was creating them? 

### Preventing a revolution 

1. What might a revolution destroy that One nation Conservatives are keen to protect at all costs? 

2. Why does Disraeli introduce new laws to protect the poor – what was he trying to give them?  

3. How are these ideas replicated after WWII? 

4. Extension – Are these ideas left-wing or right-wing? What do you think? Why? 

### Making the rich fulfil their moral obligations 

1. What does noblesse oblige translate as? What does it actually mean? 

2. From when did the idea originate? 

3. How has the Industrial Revolution made the poorer more vulnerable? 

4. Extension – From a more cynical perspective, why is morality not necessarily the prime motivator for rich to support the poor? 

### Civilisation & Other Societies 

1. What analogy do One Nation Conservatives use for the country – to describe the relationship between rich and poor? Why do they believe this? 

2. What is constructive imperialism? How is this linked to their view about the nation? 

### Controlling the Free Market 

1. In what way do One Nation Conservatives agree with Traditional Conservatives about the free market? 

2. However, why are One Nation Conservatives somewhat apprehensive about the free market? 

3. Why does Disraeli support the Corn Law, even when they hurt poorer people? 

4. Why does Disraeli intervene in the free market with health and safety legislation? (Look back at earlier in the notes) 

5. Why do One Nation Conservatives keep government intervention in the 1950s? 

6. What is the government aiming to maintain? Why? 

7. How do One Nation Conservatives seem like Social Democrats on the surface, but differ with their logic?

## Traditional conservatism questions

### Flawed Humanity: 

1. In what three ways are humans seen as flawed? 

2. For each why, why do they believe that we are flawed? And are there any implications for this? 

3. Do they believe that these flaws can be overcome? Why? 

### Conserving Institutions: 

1. What is an institution? 

2. What are the two things that institutions can be based upon? Which do Traditional Conservatives favour? Why? 

3. Institutions can be interconnected – so what might be the problem with altering one of them? 

4. What is organic development? What is the alternative way of understanding society? 

5. What did Burke say was the ‘real’ social contract? 

### Defending Hierarchy: 

1. What are the two justifications that Traditional Conservatives give for the idea of hierarchy? 

### Rights and the Use of Authority: 

1. What is the difference between a subject and a citizen? 

2. Why do Traditional Conservatives support rights? How is their justification different to that of Liberals? 

3. Why type of rights do Traditional Conservatives criticise? Why? 

4. When should authority be used, according to Traditional Conservatives? Why did they support the Peterloo Massacre? 

5. Why should people respect authority, according to Traditional Conservatives? 

6. What is the relationship between authority and rights? 

7. What is the worst case scenario for the police? 

### Defending society against ‘utopianism’ 

1. What things pose the most dangerous threat to institutions? 

2. Why will one generation likely fail to replace one institution with a brand-new better one? 

3. What does Burke criticise about the French Revolutionaries treatment of the Catholic Church? 

### Defending the Free Market: 

1. What are the two grounds that Traditional Conservatives defend the free market upon? 

2. When will Traditional Conservatives not defend the free-market? How do they differ from Classical Liberals?
