| Term                                                                                                                                            | Definition                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Parliamentary                                                                                                                                   | Trying to take control of the state through democracy, rather than revolution.                                                                                                    |
| Welfare state                                                                                                                                   | The organisation that gives out generous benefits and particularly supports the poor, looking after people from ‘cradle to grave’.                                                |
| Class                                                                                                                                           | Groups in society based loosely upon your economic position.                                                                                                                      |
| Social Justice                                                                                                                                  | Not complete equality of outcome, but a more equal distribution of wealth than you’d see under free-market capitalism. It can be justified on moral grounds as ‘fair’ or ‘right’. |
| Three problems with capitalism                                                                                                                  | Inefficiency, inequality and unpleasant human virtues                                                                                                                             |
| Mixed economy                                                                                                                                   | A balance between free-market capitalism and a planned system                                                                                                                     |
| Nationalisation                                                                                                                                 | Where the government purchases and runs a company. Usually involves the purchasing of all companies within an industry.                                                           |
| Keynesiansim                                                                                                                                    | The ideas of a liberal economist. He argued that the Wall Street Crash in 1929 had been caused by too little government control of the economy.                                   |
| Postwar Consensus                                                                                                                               | When the Conservative Party and Labour Party agreed on economic policy, after 1945. It was ended by Margaret Thatcher in 1979.                                                    |
| Price controls                                                                                                                                  | Where the government sets a ceiling or a floor for how much something can cost.                                                                                                   |
| Full employment                                                                                                                                 | Where everybody who wants a job can get a job.                                                                                                                                    |
| Crime affects class                                                                                                                             | The belief that economic conditions can encourage humans to certain things – different to Marx’s view that economics = human nature.                                              |
