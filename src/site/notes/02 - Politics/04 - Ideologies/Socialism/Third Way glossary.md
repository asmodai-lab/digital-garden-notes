| Term                            | Definition                                                                                                                                                                            |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Equality of opportunity         | Where everybody has relatively similar access to chances to succeed in life, regardless of your parents wealth or social position                                                     |
| Socially excluded               | Sometime known as the ‘precariat’, or pejoratively as ‘chavs’ or ‘the underclass’. People who have been pushed to the edge of society, usually through poverty.                       |
| 'hand up, not a hand out'       | Describes the Third Way approach to welfare. Coined by Bill Clinton.                                                                                                                  |
| Workfare                        | A scheme used commonly in the USA, where the unemployment must do training courses and work for qualifications in order to receive their benefits. Aim – get them off benefits.       |
| competition state               | Where the government tries to make the country very attractive to foreign investors, usually by joining trade blocs (like the EU), lowering taxes and highly educating its workforce. |
| community over class            | There is no class war/struggle. Everyone should try to get along with each other.                                                                                                     |
| consensus/social harmony        | Humans are social creatures, not solitary individualistic creatures.                                                                                                                  |
| communitarian liberalism        | People live in groups. Individual can enjoy their own personal rights, but they also have responsibilities to other people in their groups.                                           |
| social inclusion                | Trying to help people who have been pushed to the edge of society, often by providing them with new skills and a good education for their kids.                                       |
| rejection of state intervention | Minimal price controls, minimal nationalisation, no economic plans.                                                                                                                   |
| knowledge economy               | An economy where most people earn their salary through skills and providing services (not goods)                                                                                      |
| Claus IV                        | A part of the Labour Party constitution. Controversially changed in 1995 by Blair.                                                                                                    |
| Free market                     | Not a planned economy or mixed economy. Few regulations.                                                                                                                              |
| community minded                | Human beings no longer strongly belong to groups that are based on economic status.                                                                                                   |
| moral obligations               | It’s bad if you don’t do this.                                                                                                                                                        |
| non-dualistic worldview         | Things don’t have to be black and white, even if they’ve seemed that was in the past. They can be black and white.  Changing mindset can create greater social harmony.               |
