## Terminology

| Term               | Definition                                                                                                                                 |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Biblical authority | The extent to which the Bible influences human behaviour and morality.                                                                     |
| Theonomous ethics  | Ethics is determined by God                                                                                                                |
| Sola Scriptura     | "by scripture alone" doctrine that holds the Bible as the supreme authority in faith and practice                                          |
| Covenant           | The bible is God's promise and agreement with humans, which requires good behaviour from them.                                             |
| Hermeneutic circle | Method of interpretation that focusses on a continual, circular pattern of reading and understanding passages in light of the entire Bible |
| Heternomous ethics | Ethics are governed by several sources; the Bible, human reason, conscience                                                                |
| Papal encylical    | Letter issues by the Pope to senior clergy on a significant topic or teaching                                                              |
| Magisterium        | Authority of the Pope and bishops as the official and authentic teachings of the church                                                    |
| Natural law        | Basic moral principles set by God, discoverable by human nature.                                                                           |
| Autonomous ethics  | Ethics are self-governing and discovered through human reason alone                                                                        |
| Agape              | Universal, unconditional, sacrificial love originating from God; often seen as the ideal motivation for mral action                        |
| Sacred tradition   | Both the scripture and the church are authoritative in communicating revaluation in Christ.                                                |
