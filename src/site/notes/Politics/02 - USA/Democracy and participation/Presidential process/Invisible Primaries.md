#lessonminor 

Invisible primaries aren't even primaries.

People need lots of money to do primaries, which means they need financial backers. 

Some people are going to fall by the wayside because of the expensive nature of primaries.

*This* is the invisible primary.

#### How to be successful in an invisible primary

- have money
- have name recognition

### Why do you want to win the invisible primary?

The winner gets advantage from winning the invisible primary- by being a frontrunner. 

Also, the winner gets:
- a huge about of money for advertising
- support of major donors to get funding later on
- vocal support and endorsements from major office holders
- gained 'insider establishment' status because they are supported by the party leaders
- a sense of inevitability about your being chosen as winner
- significant media coverage

==Winning the invisible primary is a huge advantage, but not a guarantee.==

###### 2020 Democratic party primary

There were 29 notable figures running for the democratic primary. Over half withdrew before the first primaries- some of which were big names but didn't manage to get momentum.
- For example, Kamala Harris was one of them.
- BUT Pete Buttigieg, mostly unknown guy, did really well in the invisible primary (he was clear, former soldier, and gay). He wins the Iowa caucus, despite being almost unknown beforehand.
	- But he came up against Bernie Sanders (who he disliked because he was a centrist)
	- so then a bunch drop out to support Biden in order to beat Bernie
