## How can the stages in the election cycle affect a president's power? 

[[Essay plans/Politics Essay Plans/Older essay plans/Examine the extent to which presidents experience different degrees of  power\|Debate]] 

| Stages in the election cycle         | Typically characterized by:                                                                                                                                  |
| ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Soon after the presidential election | Presidents are at their most powerful and influential. They have a clear mandate- and congress will generally support them because they are popular.         |
| Mid-term elections                   | Common to lose control of at least one house in Congress, massively reducing their ability to achieve policy goals. President must work on being persuasive. |
| Throughout the remainder of the term | Popularity often declines, making persuasion in Congress even harder. Policies goals may have not been achieved, and scandals build up.                      |
| The end of a president's second term | The president sometimes becomes a 'lame duck' in the last 6-12 months as everyone is focused on their successors.                                            |
